# Positioning Examples & Teardowns

Concrete examples to anchor the abstract framework in `KNOWLEDGE.md`.
When you draft positioning, compare against these before returning.

---

## Example: a BAD value prop for tax-contester (rejected)

> "AI-powered property tax intelligence platform leveraging machine
> learning to optimize your assessment outcomes."

Why it fails:
- Category ("intelligence platform") doesn't match how homeowners search.
- "Leveraging", "optimize", "outcomes" — corporate noise.
- No mention of Iowa, Polk, Dallas, or the April 30 deadline.
- No competitive alternative implied — could be selling anything.
- Promises an "outcome" we cannot guarantee.

---

## Example: a STRONG value prop shape

> "Free protest evidence for Polk & Dallas County homeowners — built from
> real assessor sales data, ready to file before the April 30 deadline."

Why it works:
- **Category:** "protest evidence" (concrete, matches mental model).
- **Audience:** named counties.
- **Alternative implied:** paying a firm for the same evidence.
- **Differentiator:** "real assessor sales data" — proof not adjective.
- **Urgency:** the deadline.
- **Anti-pattern avoided:** no "guarantee", no "AI".

## Example: filling out the Dunford five steps

### 1. Competitive alternatives

| Alternative | What homeowner does today | Where it falls short |
|---|---|---|
| Do nothing | Pays the assessed bill | Leaves $200–$800/yr on the table; compounds |
| Self-appeal cold | Walks into Board of Review with a complaint | High effort, low win rate, no data |
| Hire a 30%-firm | Pro files for them | Loses 30–50% of savings; minimum fees can exceed small wins |
| Zillow + spreadsheet | DIY with public data | Zillow comps aren't recognized by the Board; no §441.37 framing |

### 2. Unique attributes

- Real Polk CAMA + recorder sales (173K parcels, 381K sales)
- 4-pass weighted comp scoring
- 2-page packet citing Iowa Code §441.37
- Built specifically around the Polk/Dallas Board of Review process
- Free, no contingency

### 3. Value translation

| Attribute | Customer outcome |
|---|---|
| Real assessor sales data | "These aren't Zillow guesses — these are sales the Board already knows about" |
| 4-pass scoring | "Comps that actually match your house, not the foreclosure two streets over" |
| §441.37 citation | "Files in the format the Board expects" |
| Free | "Keep 100% of what you win" |
| Iowa-specific | "Speaks the deadline, the form, the body" |

### 4. Best-fit customer

DIY homeowner, Polk/Dallas, single-family, comfortable with a web form,
suspicious of 30%-firms. Disqualifiers: commercial, multi-state,
income-valued.

### 5. Market category

**"Free protest evidence for homeowners"** (not "AI tax software", not
"appeal service").

## Teardown: what3words (cited in the Reddit thread that triggered this project)

- **Category:** emergency location communication.
- **Alternative:** lat/long, "near the gas station".
- **Unique:** 3-word grid covering the whole earth.
- **Value:** precise location in a panic.
- **Best-fit:** hikers, emergency dispatchers.
- **Failure mode:** misheard words ("south" vs "mouth") — anti-pattern
  for emergencies, which is why the Reddit thread rejected it for the
  iPhone-app use case.

**Lesson for tax-contester:** even good positioning has failure modes;
name them honestly in the "What this is NOT" section of BRAND.md so the
positioning is robust to scrutiny.

## Teardown: TurboTax (peak-season hero)

> "Get every dollar you deserve."

- Specific outcome ("every dollar"), time-bound by April 15.
- Loss aversion: implies money is being left on the table.
- No category jargon. Anyone reading it knows what to do next.

**Lesson for tax-contester:** mirror this on the April 30 cycle. The
deadline IS the hero.
