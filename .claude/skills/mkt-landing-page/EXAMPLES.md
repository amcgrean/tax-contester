# Landing Page Examples & Swipe File

Reference heroes and section patterns. Compare drafts against these
before returning to the user.

## Strong hero examples (general)

### Linear
> "Linear is a purpose-built tool for planning and building products."

Names the tool, names the user, names the job. No fluff.

### Basecamp
> "It's about damn time."

Headline names a feeling; subhead names the audience and outcome.
Permission to be opinionated.

### Stripe
> "Financial infrastructure for the internet."

Category framing as positioning. Defines a new shelf with one phrase.

## Section examples that earn their slot

### "How it works" (3 steps)

Each step gets one short verb-phrase + a screenshot of the actual UI.

1. **Find your parcel.** Type your address — fuzzy autocomplete finds it.
2. **See your comps.** Real sales near you, scored against your home.
3. **Download your packet.** 2-page PDF, citing Iowa Code §441.37,
   ready for the Board of Review.

### "What you get"

A picture of the 2-page packet PDF (real, not a mockup) with three
callouts:

- "Comp table the Board recognizes"
- "§441.37 grounds laid out"
- "Signature block + relief requested"

### "Why this is free" (the trust-builder paragraph)

> Built by an Iowan who got tired of watching neighbors hand 30% of
> their savings to appeal firms. The data is public. The Board of
> Review process is public. The only thing missing was a tool that put
> them together. So here it is — free.

(Short. Honest. No "Series A coming" or "future paid plans". If
monetization arrives, rewrite this section as the first move.)

### "What this is NOT" (the rare power section)

> - **Not a tax attorney.** We don't represent you at the hearing.
> - **Not a guarantee.** The Board of Review can rule against you.
> - **Not for commercial property** or income-valued buildings.
> - **Not statewide** (yet). Polk + Dallas counties only.

This section converts skeptics. Most sites are afraid to write it.

### FAQ (6 questions max, real objections only)

- "How do I know the comps are accurate?"
- "What if my assessment is actually fair?"
- "Will the assessor be mad at me?"
- "What happens after I file the packet?"
- "Why is this free?"
- "Do you work outside Polk and Dallas?"

Reject these (corporate filler):
- "What is Tax Contester?"
- "How do I get started?"
- "Is my data secure?" (only include if the answer is non-obvious)

## Hero patterns to NEVER ship

- "Take control of your property taxes." — lifestyle, no specificity.
- "AI-powered tax intelligence." — voice violation + meaningless.
- "Save thousands today!" — promises savings, exclamation point, vague.
- "The future of property tax appeals." — future tense, no audience.
- "We make property tax appeals easy." — first-person "we", no value.

## Mobile-viewport sanity check

On a 375px viewport, each hero variant should fit:

- Headline: ≤ 60 chars (wraps to 2 lines max)
- Subhead: ≤ 140 chars (wraps to 3 lines max)
- CTA button: full-width, 48px tall minimum

If a draft headline exceeds 60 chars, rewrite — don't ship a 3-line
hero on mobile.
