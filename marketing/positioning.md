## Market category

We sit on the shelf labeled: **Free protest evidence for homeowners**

Not "AI tax software" (wrong audience, wrong expectations) and not "appeal
service" (implies a human agent). "Protest evidence" matches what homeowners
Google and matches the exact job: show up to the Board with something real.

## Competitive alternatives

| Alternative | What it does | Where it falls short |
|---|---|---|
| Do nothing | Pays the assessed bill | $200–$800/yr left on the table; compounds over the 2-year assessment cycle |
| Self-appeal cold | Walks into the Board of Review with a complaint and no comps | High effort, low credibility — the Board expects evidence |
| Hire a 30–50%-of-savings firm | Pro files for you | Takes 30–50% of savings; minimum fees ($150–$300) can exceed small wins entirely |
| Zillow + own spreadsheet | DIY with public estimates | Zillow comps aren't recorder-sourced; Board doesn't accept them as comparable sales |

## Unique attributes

1. Official Polk CAMA + recorder sales (173K parcels, 381K arms-length
   sales) — not estimates, not scraped from a listing site
2. 4-pass weighted comp scoring: sqft ±20%, age ±15 yrs, same neighborhood
   first, sale recency weighted — matches your house, not the nearest
   listing
3. 2-page print-ready packet citing Iowa Code §441.37, formatted the way
   the Polk County Board of Review expects
4. Free — no contingency cut, no subscription, no minimum fee
5. Built around the Iowa protest calendar — April 30 deadline, Board of
   Review, §441.37 grounds — not a national tool retrofitted to Iowa

## Best-fit customer

**Primary:** DIY homeowner, 35–70, Polk or Dallas County, single-family
home, received a 2026 assessment notice that feels wrong. Comfortable
with a web form. Not willing to pay a contingency firm for a $300 win.
Wants to do something real before the April 30 deadline but won't hire
an attorney.

**Disqualifiers (we will NOT chase):**
- Commercial property owners
- Income-valued properties (apartments, rental portfolios)
- Multi-county or multi-state portfolios
- Anyone outside Polk or Dallas County
- Anyone who has already missed the Board of Review deadline

## Messaging pillars

### Pillar 1: Real data — the same sales the Board already has

The Board of Review doesn't care about Zillow estimates. They care about
recorded sales of comparable homes. Tax Contester pulls from the same
official Polk CAMA and recorder data the Board uses.

- Proof: 173K Polk parcels from official county CAMA data
- Proof: 381K arms-length sales from the Polk County recorder
- Proof: Comps are filtered for arms-length, recency (24–36 months),
  and matching property class — the same filters the Board applies

### Pillar 2: A packet the Board can act on, not a complaint form

Filing a protest without comps is a complaint. Filing with comps,
a §441.37 citation, and a structured argument is a protest the Board
can actually evaluate and rule on.

- Proof: 2-page print-ready packet (save as PDF, file the same day)
- Proof: Cites Iowa Code §441.37 explicitly — the statutory ground
  for assessment protests
- Proof: Comp table formatted with address, sale price, price-per-sqft,
  and similarity score — the format the Board expects

### Pillar 3: Free — keep 100% of what you win

The 30%-of-savings firms are a rational choice when the win is large.
When the win is $400, paying $120–$200 to a firm isn't. Tax Contester
costs nothing. If you win $400, you keep $400.

- Proof: $0 to use, no account required, no credit card
- Proof: No contingency cut, no minimum fee, no subscription
- Proof: Built by an Iowan who got tired of the 30%-firms taking
  a cut of savings homeowners earned themselves

## Anti-positioning (things we will NOT say)

- **"AI-powered"** — not a differentiator for a homeowner who wants
  evidence, and invites skepticism from the Board-of-Review audience
- **"Guaranteed savings"** — the Board can rule against any protest;
  promising an outcome is both dishonest and legally risky
- **"Disrupting the property tax industry"** — Polk County homeowners
  don't want disruption; they want to pay a fair bill
- **"Leverage your assessment data"** — "leverage" and "optimize" are
  corporate noise; replace with plain verbs (check, file, contest)
- **We don't name the 30%-firms by name** — speak to the category
  ("contingency firms") not individual brands; stays professional and
  avoids legal exposure
- **We don't claim a win rate** — no published data yet; claiming one
  would be fabricated

## 10-second test

A first-time visitor to tax-contester.vercel.app should answer in 10 sec:

- **What is this?** A free tool that finds comparable sales and builds
  a protest packet for your Polk or Dallas County property assessment.
- **Who is it for?** Iowa homeowners who got an assessment notice that
  feels too high and want to contest it before April 30.
- **Why should I care?** It uses real recorder data — not Zillow — and
  produces a Board-ready packet for free.
